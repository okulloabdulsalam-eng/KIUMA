Islamic Values & Beliefs

The foundation of our association

Core Islamic Principles

KIUMA is built upon these fundamental Islamic values and beliefs

Tawheed

The Oneness of Allah (SWT) - the foundation of Islamic faith. We believe in the absolute unity and uniqueness of Allah, with no partners, associates, or equals.

"Say: He is Allah, the One and Only" (Quran 112:1)

üìñ

Quran & Sunnah

We follow the Quran as the word of Allah and the authentic Sunnah of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) as our primary sources of guidance and legislation.

"And We have sent down to you the Book as clarification for all things" (Quran 16:89)

Akhlaaq (Character)

We emphasize the development of excellent character and moral conduct, following the example of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him), who was sent to perfect good character.

"The best among you are those who have the best character" (Hadith)

ü§ù

Brotherhood

We believe in the unity of the Muslim Ummah and the importance of brotherhood and sisterhood in Islam, regardless of nationality, ethnicity, or background.

"The believers are but brothers" (Quran 49:10)

üìö

Knowledge Seeking

We believe in the importance of seeking knowledge, both Islamic and worldly, as a form of worship and a means to serve Allah and humanity.

"Seek knowledge from the cradle to the grave" (Hadith)

üíö

Service & Charity

We believe in serving the community, helping those in need, and engaging in charitable activities as a means of worship and fulfilling our Islamic duty.

"The best of people are those who are most beneficial to others" (Hadith)

Our Commitment

At KIUMA, we are committed to upholding these Islamic values in all our activities, programs, and interactions. We strive to create an environment where these principles are not just taught, but lived and practiced daily.
